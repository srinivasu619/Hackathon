Reason for less Female Literacy Ratio

  • Boys (Gross Enrollment Ratio) - 123.33
  • Girls (Gross Enrollment Ratio) - 106.62
  • Boys (Dropout Ratio) - 32.73
  • Girls (Dropout Ratio) - 36.14

From the above stats we can see that the enrollment ratio of girls is low and girls Dropout Ratio is higher this relation is one of the factor of the low literacy rate among Gender(Female)

Expenditure on the Education vs PerCapita Income

  • Tamil Nadu, spends Rs. 4185 per capita on education and has a whopping perCapita income of over Rs. 2,00,000.
  • Bihar spends less than Rs. 1800 per capita and has a per capita income of Rs 31,000

Usually, PerCapita spending on education depicts the literacy rate and this inturn affects the PerCapita Income

Government Schemes for Literacy Improvement

  • Rural Female Literacy Rate(2003) - 48%
  • Rural Female Literacy Rate(2011) - 59%

Government Schemes

To promote enrolment of girl child in the age group of 14-18 at secondary stage, especially those who passed Class VIII and to encourage the secondary education of such girls, the Centrally Sponsored Scheme. National Scheme of Incentives to Girls for Secondary Education was launched in May, 2008

Infrastructure Development in Minority Institutes (IDMI) - IDMI has been operationalisedto augment Infrastructure in Private Aided/Unaided Minority Schools/Institutions in order to enhance quality of education to minority children.

Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya - 2004 - Educational facilities (residential schools) for girls belonging to SC, ST, OBC, minority communities and families below the poverty line(BPL) in Educationally Backward Blocks
